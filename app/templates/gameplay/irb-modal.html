I. Purpose of this Research Project

You are being asked to participate in a research study. The purpose of this study is to understand how people can make graph visualizations easier to understand. The researchers will ask you to use our software tool to look at graphs and create new layouts for them using some guidelines. The results of this study may be published in academic conferences or journals or included in student theses and dissertations. We are recruiting about 1000 adult men and women from around the world for this section of the study. You must be at least 18 years old to participate.

II. Procedures

If you agree to participate, we will ask you to do the following activities:
  • Use our software to create new layouts for graphs of biological data.
The entire procedure will take place online. It should take less than 2 minutes to complete the HIT and 20 minutes to complete the bonus task (optional).

III. Risks

The risks of the study are minimal and are similar to what you would encounter doing everyday computer tasks like searching the Internet, writing a document, or checking email.

IV. Benefits

You will not receive any benefits by participating in this study. However, the study will help us develop new techniques for helping people understand complex graph data. This could benefit society by helping scientists understand biological data that leads to new medical discoveries. It could also help other kinds of professionals who work with graph data. No promise or guarantee of benefits has been made to encourage you to participate.

V. Extent of Anonymity and Confidentiality

We may share your anonymized study data in publications or in data sets on our project website. If we use your data for these purposes, we will use the numeric code instead of your name to refer to you. We may also aggregate your data with other participants when reporting our results to minimize the possibility of identifying you. Your personal information will never be shared outside the research team. The Virginia Tech (VT) Institutional Review Board (IRB) may view the study’s data for auditing purposes. The IRB is responsible for the oversight of the protection of human subjects involved in research.

VI. Compensation

You will be compensated $7.25 per hour for your participation in the HIT. Payment will be sent to you via Amazon Mechanical Turk.

For the bonus task, you will be compensated only for your performance. Higher the score, higher the bonus. Bonus will be sent to you via Amazon Mechanical Turk as a bonus.

VII. Freedom to Withdraw

It is important for you to know that you are free to withdraw from this study at any time without penalty. You are free not to answer any questions that you choose or respond to what is being asked of you without penalty. Please note that there may be circumstances under which the investigator may determine that a subject should not continue as a subject. Should you withdraw or otherwise discontinue participation, you will be compensated for the portion of the project completed in accordance with the Compensation section of this document.

VIII. Questions or Concerns

Should you have any questions about this study, you may contact one of the research investigators whose contact information is included at the beginning of this document. Should you have any questions or concerns about the study’s conduct or your rights as a research participant, or need to report a research-related injury or event, you may contact the Virginia Tech Institutional Review Board at irb@vt.edu or (540) 231-3732.
